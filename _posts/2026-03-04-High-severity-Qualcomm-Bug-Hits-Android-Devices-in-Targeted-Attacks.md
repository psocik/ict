## High-severity Qualcomm Bug Hits Android Devices in Targeted Attacks 🚨

Google has patched **129 vulnerabilities** in Android in its March 2026 Android Security Bulletin, including a Qualcomm display flaw that is known to be actively exploited. If your Android phone shows a patch level of **2026-03-05** or later, these issues are fixed.

The Android zero-day, tracked as **CVE-2026-21385**, is a high-severity bug in a Qualcomm graphics/display component that attackers are already exploiting in limited, targeted attacks. This vulnerability affects a large number of Android chipsets, with Qualcomm listing over **230 different chipset models** that are impacted. It is reasonable to assume that this issue affects **hundreds of millions of devices** worldwide.

### What You Need to Know 🛡️
- The vulnerability lives in an open-source Qualcomm graphics/display component.
- Attackers need to trick users into installing a malicious app to compromise the device.
- Always install apps from official app stores and verify the developer’s name, number of downloads, and user reviews before installation.
- Scrutinize app permissions, especially if they request access to sensitive features like **camera** or **SMS**.

### Stay Safe! 🔒
Keep your Android, Google Play services, and all other important apps up to date to ensure you receive the latest security fixes.
