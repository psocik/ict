## Defense Contractor Employee Jailed for Selling 8 Zero-Days to Russian Broker

The nature of the exploits is presently unclear. However, a sentencing memorandum published earlier this month revealed that the tools could have been used against any manner of victim, civilian or military around the world, engaging in all manner of crime from **cyber fraud**, **theft**, and **ransomware**, to state-directed spying and offensive cyber operations against military targets. üåç

Operation Zero has offered up to **$1 million** in bounties for Telegram exploits and **$500 million** for tools that could be used to break into Android and iPhone devices. The exploit broker is believed to have engaged in efforts to recruit hackers to support its activities and develop business relationships with foreign intelligence agencies through social media.
