## Exciting News for Flipper Zero Users! 🚀

Flipper Devices has announced that the development of the **Flipper Zero firmware** will continue, albeit with a smaller internal team and a greater reliance on community contributions. This decision comes as the gadget maker shifts its focus towards building new devices, such as the **Flipper One** open Linux platform, which also seeks community assistance for its development.

While the official firmware for the **Flipper Zero** portable pen-testing device will still be maintained, full-time feature development has officially concluded. The first major stable release, **Flipper Zero Firmware 1.0**, was announced in September 2024 after three years of dedicated development. The latest stable release, version **1.4.3**, has been available since December 2025, marking a significant milestone in the firmware's maturity with a stable SDK and APIs.

In recent discussions, the Flipper Devices team addressed concerns that firmware development had halted, leading to a strong backlash from the community. To address these concerns, the team has developed a new approach that emphasizes closer interaction with contributors to keep firmware development progressing.

### New Development Approach 📅
- **Weekly Evaluations:** Flipper Zero requests will now be evaluated weekly.
- **GitHub Discussions:** All communication will occur through GitHub Discussions, where new requests will be voted on.
- **Community Pull Requests:** Accepted with stricter review requirements.
- **Testing Requirements:** Firmware changes will require mandatory integration and regression testing, open to community participation.

The development team will maintain oversight and pay special attention to AI-generated code that interacts with low-level functions and changes affecting the user interface or documentation. With over **one million Flipper Zero users**, the volume of communication has become challenging for the small team to manage, leading to the disabling of direct messages on social media channels.

All requests will now be submitted to GitHub Discussions and prioritized based on community votes, empowering users to influence the future of Flipper Zero.
