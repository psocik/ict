## 21,786 Home Cameras Exposed: No Password, No Warning

In May 2026, Mysterium VPN conducted a thorough investigation of public internet devices and discovered a staggering **21,786 home cameras** streaming live video without any password protection or warnings! 🚨 This alarming finding is part of a larger count of over **three million** reachable devices on the open internet.

### Key Findings:
- **No Login Required:** The researchers found that none of these devices required any hacking or password entry to access the live feeds.
- **Dominant Brands:** Two brands, **Hikvision** and **Dahua**, dominate the market, with Hikvision cameras being open just **0.06%** of the time, while Dahua cameras were effectively never open due to mandatory activation policies.
- **Budget Devices Vulnerable:** Budget recorders, particularly those classified as **"HiSilicon-class"**, were found to be open **27.1%** of the time, with a legacy webcam application called **webcamXP** showing an alarming **45.6%** openness.

### Security Implications:
An open feed poses significant risks, revealing when a home is empty and detailing the daily routines of its inhabitants. This information can be aggregated and exploited by malicious actors. The **Mirai botnet** of 2016 demonstrated how easily default passwords can be used to create vast attack networks.

### Recommendations for Homeowners:
- **Set Strong Passwords:** Always set a real password for your devices.
- **Disable UPnP:** Turn off Universal Plug and Play on your router.
- **Control RTSP Streams:** Disable any RTSP streams that you haven't configured yourself.
- **Update Firmware:** Regularly update the firmware of your devices.
- **Consider Encrypted Connections:** Prefer devices that connect through encrypted relays to avoid open ports.

By taking these steps, you can significantly enhance your home security and protect your privacy. 🛡️
