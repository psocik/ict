## 🚨 Data Breach Alert: 6.9 Million Driver's License Numbers Stolen!

Insurance provider **AssuranceAmerica** has confirmed a significant data breach affecting the personal information and driver's license numbers of up to **6.9 million** individuals. AssuranceAmerica provides car and rental insurance across **14 US states** through a network of over **9,500 independent agents**.

### What Happened?
AssuranceAmerica discovered hackers infiltrating its computer systems on **March 17**. Following an investigation that concluded on **June 15**, it was revealed that hackers had stolen customers' names, contact information, and driver's license numbers. The breach notice also indicates that information about customers' auto insurance policies, accounts, drivers, vehicles, and claims details were compromised.

### How Did This Occur?
The breach reportedly began with a targeted **phishing attack** against a single employee. An unauthorized third party accessed parts of the insurer's IT systems and copied files containing sensitive customer policy information and driver's license numbers. As of now, there has been no public link to a specific threat group, ransomware operation, or nation-state actor, and no ransom demands have been reported.

### Protect Yourself! 🛡️
After a data breach, it's crucial to take protective measures:
- **Change your passwords**: Make stolen passwords useless by updating them.
- **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)**: Use a FIDO2-compliant device for added security.
- **Watch out for impersonation scams**: Verify any communication from the company through different channels.
- **Consider identity monitoring**: This can alert you if your personal information is being traded illegally online.
