## Apple Fixes WebKit Flaws in iOS and macOS 🚀

Apple has released crucial updates for **iOS**, **iPadOS**, **macOS**, and **Safari**, addressing several WebKit flaws. Notably, four of these vulnerabilities were identified using advanced AI tools like Claude and Codex. This marks a significant shift in how security vulnerabilities are being discovered and addressed.

### Key Updates 🔧
- Apple patched four critical bugs in WebKit, the engine responsible for rendering web content on Apple devices.
- The advisory lists nearly **30 fixes** across WebKit, including a use-after-free vulnerability and a flaw that allowed malicious websites to access restricted content.
- On the kernel side, three separate bugs could have led to serious issues, such as leaking kernel state or crashing the system.

### Why the Urgency? ⏳
Apple is pushing these fixes out ahead of schedule due to the rapid pace at which AI can exploit known vulnerabilities. Typically, security updates are bundled with broader software releases, but this time, Apple is prioritizing security to protect users from potential threats.

### Conclusion 🔒
The latest updates are now live: **iOS 26.5.2**, **iPadOS 26.5.2**, **macOS Tahoe 26.5.2**, and **Safari 26.5.2**. Apple assures that none of the patched vulnerabilities were exploited before the fixes were released. If you haven't updated your devices yet, now is the perfect time to do so!
