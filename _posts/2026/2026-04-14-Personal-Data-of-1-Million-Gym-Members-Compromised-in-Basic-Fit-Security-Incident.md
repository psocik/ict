## 🚨 Personal Data Breach Alert!

Basic-Fit, Europe's largest gym chain, has disclosed a **data breach** affecting around **1 million members**. Hackers gained unauthorized access to the company's systems and stole personal data. The gym chain reported that it recently detected the intrusion and is actively investigating the incident while notifying affected users and working to contain the impact.

> "Today, Basic-Fit has notified the relevant data protection authority concerning unauthorized access to the system that records members' visits to Basic-Fit clubs," reads the press release published by the company.

The unauthorized access was detected by their system monitoring processes and was stopped within minutes of discovery. The members whose data is involved have been informed. 

External experts found that intruders downloaded some data from Basic-Fit, affecting members in several countries, including about **200,000** in the Netherlands. According to the company's press release:

> "An investigation conducted by external security experts has shown that some of the data stored in the system was downloaded. The downloaded data concerns active members in several countries. In the Netherlands, around **200,000** members are affected. The data concerns membership information, name and address details, email addresses, phone numbers, dates of birth, and bank account details. Basic-Fit does not hold identification documents of members and no passwords were accessed."

There is currently no evidence of misuse, and the company continues to monitor the situation with external specialists. The press release further states:

> "The investigation so far has not shown the data being available anywhere or having been misused. Together with external specialists, Basic-Fit continues to monitor the issue closely."

It's unclear who carried out the attack, and at this time no ransomware group has claimed responsibility for the security breach. Basic-Fit is one of Europe's largest fitness operators, with over **5 million members** and more than **1,600 clubs** across **6 countries**.
