## INC Ransomware Group Holds Healthcare Hostage in Oceania 🚨

Cybersecurity authorities in Oceania are warning that the INC ransomware operation has been wreaking havoc on healthcare organizations in the region. INC is targeting the industry at a higher rate than almost any other ransomware group.

On March 6, the Australian Cyber Security Centre (ACSC), the Kingdom of Tonga's National Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT Tonga), and New Zealand's National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) released a joint advisory about this threat. The advisory primarily focused on INC's targeting of critical networks in the healthcare sector.

Initially, INC focused on the US and UK, but by the summer of 2024, it began targeting Australian companies in professional services and healthcare. This activity escalated in 2025, expanding into neighboring New Zealand and Tonga. Tonga experienced a significant attack from INC on June 15, 2025, disrupting national health services.

The ACSC responded to 11 INC ransomware attacks in Australia between July 2024 and December 2025, predominantly affecting healthcare and professional services companies. Attackers typically gained access to victims by purchasing compromised accounts from initial access brokers (IABs) or through spear-phishing and exploiting known vulnerabilities in Internet-facing devices.

INC operates on a ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS) model, allowing its tactics to vary. After gaining access, attackers moved laterally within networks, escalating privileges before deploying their ransomware and ransom notes. In some cases, they exfiltrated personally identifiable information (PII) and protected health information (PHI).

In New Zealand, INC stole a large amount of data and encrypted several servers and endpoint devices at a healthcare organization, later publishing the stolen data on its Dark Web leak site. Authorities have identified a specific hacker behind the Tonga attack — Roman Khubov, known online as "blackod".

Oceania cyber authorities recommend that organizations defend against the INC ransomware group by taking basic cybersecurity precautions, such as monitoring and restricting network traffic and remote access, implementing multifactor authentication (MFA) where applicable, and diligently managing software vulnerabilities.

Christopher Hills, chief security strategist at BeyondTrust, states, "INC is not employing new or cutting-edge tactics to compromise this industry; instead, they are using what I refer to as legacy tactics to compromise organizations." He added that "These threat actors are walking right into the environments with valid credentials."
