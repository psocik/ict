## A Foreign Hacker Accessed FBI Files on Epstein Back in 2023 - Report

New reporting claims that some FBI files tied to the Epstein investigation were accessed by a foreign hacker all the way back in 2023. 🕵️‍♂️ Reuters reported today, citing an unnamed source and recently released Justice Department documents, that a foreign cybercriminal accessed the files during a breach at the bureau’s New York Field Office. The breach itself was first reported by Reuters and CNN last month, but new details about who accessed the system and how the intrusion happened were reported today.

The hack took place after a server at the Child Exploitation Forensic Lab in the FBI’s New York Field Office was reportedly left vulnerable by Special Agent Aaron Spivack, according to Reuters. A timeline written by Spivack, reviewed by Reuters, and included in the released Epstein files, shows that the hack occurred on **February 12, 2023**. It was discovered the next day when Spivack found a text file warning that the network had been compromised.

An investigation later found traces of unusual activity, including someone “combing through certain files pertaining to the Epstein investigation,” according to the timeline. Reuters’s unnamed source claims the hacker did not realize they had breached a government server and even threatened to report the server’s owner to the FBI after encountering child abuse images. The agency was eventually able to convince the hacker that they were speaking with the Bureau after inviting them to a video chat and showing their credentials. 📞

The FBI did not immediately respond to a request for comment from Gizmodo, but it told Reuters that the “cyber incident” was ”an isolated one.” “The FBI restricted access to the malicious actor and rectified the network,” the bureau added. “The investigation remains ongoing, so we do not have further comments to provide at this time.” The incident also serves as a reminder that not even the FBI is immune to cyber break-ins. As hackers become more sophisticated, breaches involving government systems raise concerns about how sensitive investigative files could be accessed and potentially exploited by malicious actors. ⚠️
