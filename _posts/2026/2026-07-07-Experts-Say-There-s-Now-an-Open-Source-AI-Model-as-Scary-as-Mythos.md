## Experts Say There’s Now an Open Source AI Model as Scary as Mythos

🚨 **A New Threat Emerges!** 🚨

Hackers are rapidly gaining access to new AI tools that can be used for malicious purposes. According to a report by Forbes, a Beijing-based AI company, Z.ai, has launched an open-weight model named **GLM-5.2**. This model is capable of performing large-scale coding tasks and competes with Anthropic’s latest models in identifying vulnerabilities.

### Key Features of GLM-5.2:
- **Accessibility:** Anyone can download it.
- **Compatibility:** It can run on virtually any hardware.
- **No Middleman:** Unlike Mythos or Fable, there’s no vendor acting as a middleman between the AI models and users, significantly raising the cybersecurity stakes.

Consultants have indicated that hackers are already trading **GLM-5.2 jailbreaks** on Russian-language forums, highlighting the very real threat it poses. 

> “An attacker can run it locally without safety guardrails, fine-tune it against their specific targets, and operate with zero visibility to any provider or defender,” said **Travis Lanham**, CTO and founder of AI cybersecurity firm Armadin, in an interview with Axios.
